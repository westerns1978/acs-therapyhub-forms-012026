# For the Therapist (Karen)

You have the **Therapist** role — the clinical view. You can work with clients,
the schedule, forms, and treatment plans. A few Director-only areas (like
**Settings**) and a few trial-disabled areas won't appear for you, and that's
expected.

## What's in your menu

- **Dashboard** — your daily snapshot.
- **Clients** — find and open any client record.
- **Calendar** — the appointment schedule.
- **Messages** — the communication center.
- **Forms** — assign forms and review submissions.
- **Treatment Plan Library** — ready-made plan templates to apply to clients.
- **Compliance Risk** — a monitor of client alerts.

You won't see **Settings** — that's the Director's area.

## The client's Treatment Plan tab

When you open a client, you'll see tabs across the top: **Overview, Documents,
Forms, Sessions,** and **Treatment Plan**. That last tab is for clinical roles
like yours (the Office role doesn't see it). From there you can view a client's
active plan, **Edit** it, **Archive** it, or start a **New plan**. See
[Apply a treatment plan](06-common-tasks.md#apply-a-treatment-plan).

## Early AI helpers — please try them and tell us

Two AI features are switched **on** for you during the trial. They're early
experiments — try them with **made-up information** and tell us how they felt.

- **Clara** — the round assistant button in the bottom-right of every screen.
  Type a question to it. See [Finding your way around](02-navigation.md).
- **Clinical Smart Note Studio** — open it from the top **"Schedule"** menu →
  **"Create Note."** A space to draft a session note with AI help.

> ⚠️ **Sample data only.** These features send text to an AI service, so please
> don't enter real client information while testing them during the trial.

## Turned off during the trial

**Financials**, **Analytics**, and **AI Documents** are intentionally hidden for
now (see [Help & what's next](07-help-and-whats-next.md)).

> 💬 **Tell us what you think:** You'll spend the most time in the clinical
> screens — Clients, Treatment Plans, and Forms. If a step feels clunky or a
> label is confusing, that's exactly what we want to hear.
