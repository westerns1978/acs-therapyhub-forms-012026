## Welcome

Welcome! ACS TherapyHub is the online home base for the day-to-day work of
Assessment & Counseling Solutions — your clients, your schedule, your forms, and
your treatment plans, all in one place. You open it in a web browser and sign in;
there is nothing to install.

This guide is here to help you get comfortable on your own during the **30-day
trial**, *before* we sit down together to fine-tune things. You don't need any
technical background. Read what's useful, skip what isn't, and click around — you
can't break anything.

### This is a trial, so please explore (and tell us what you think)

The trial has two goals:

1. **Get you logged in and looking around** so the app feels familiar.
2. **Gather your honest reactions.** What's clear? What's confusing? What's
   missing? Your feedback during these 30 days directly shapes how we set the
   app up for your practice.

Throughout this guide you'll see **"Tell us what you think"** notes — gentle
nudges to jot down a reaction and pass it along.

### One important rule for the trial: use make-believe information

> ⚠️ **Please don't type real client names or real personal details into the app
> during the trial.** Use made-up, sample information instead (for example,
> "Sam Sample," case number "TEST-001"). This is especially important for the
> AI helpers. We'll switch to real records together later, once setup is
> complete.

When this guide names a button or label, it's written **exactly as it appears on
screen** (for example, the **"Add Client"** button), so you can match it to what
you're looking at.

## Getting started: signing in

When you open ACS TherapyHub, the first thing you'll see is the sign-in screen.
It shows the ACS logo, the title **"ACS TherapyHub"**, and the line
*"Clinical operations for Assessment & Counseling Solutions."*

There are **two ways to sign in**. During the trial, the **demo access** option
is the quickest, because it doesn't need a registered phone.

### Option A — Demo access (easiest for the trial)

1. On the sign-in screen, click **"Continue with demo access"**.
2. You'll see a short list headed **"Enter the demo as"** with three choices.
   Each is a role with a name and a one-line description:
   - **Director** — David Yoder — *"Full access — clinical, financial, settings."*
   - **Therapist** — Karen — *"Clinical work — notes, treatment plans, sessions."*
   - **Admin** — Jessica — *"Office work — intake, scheduling, billing."*
3. Click the card that matches you. You'll go straight to your **Dashboard** —
   no phone needed.

> 💡 Want to see what a teammate sees? Sign out and come back in as a different
> role. To return to the phone sign-in, click **"Back to sign in"**.

### Option B — Phone sign-in with iVALT (the secure everyday method)

This is how you'll normally sign in once your phone number is set up. Instead of
a password, you approve the sign-in on your phone with a face/biometric check
through the **iVALT** app.

1. In the box labeled **"Phone — used for sign-in approval"**, type your
   **10-digit mobile number**. (A **"+1"** is already shown for you.)
2. Click **"Sign in to ACS TherapyHub"**.
3. A secure window titled **"iVALT SECURE"** appears and asks you to
   **"Check your phone now."**
4. On your phone, approve the request with iVALT's face scan.
5. When it succeeds, the window turns green and reads **"ACCESS GRANTED"**, and
   you're taken to your **Dashboard**.

If you need to stop partway through, click **"TERMINATE HANDSHAKE"**. If a
sign-in doesn't go through, you'll see a **"Retry"** button to try again.

**If something doesn't work**

- **"Enter a 10-digit phone number registered with iVALT."** — The number wasn't
  exactly 10 digits. Re-type it and try again.
- **"...this phone number is not registered to a TherapyHub account. Contact your
  administrator."** — Your phone approved, but it isn't on file yet. Reach out to
  your contact (see [Help and what's next](#help-and-whats-next)) to get your
  number added. In the meantime, use **demo access** (Option A).

### Signing out

- Open the menu by clicking your **profile picture** at the top-right, then click
  **"Logout"**, or
- Click **"Sign Out"** at the bottom of the left-hand menu.

> 🔒 The **"Are you a client? Log in here"** link at the bottom of the screen is
> for *clients*, not staff. You can ignore it.

## Finding your way around

Once you sign in, the screen has three constant parts no matter where you go:

- a **menu down the left side** (your main navigation),
- a **bar across the top** (search, quick actions, and your profile), and
- a **round "Clara" button** floating in the bottom-right corner.

> 📱 **On a phone or small screen,** the left menu is tucked away. Tap the
> **menu icon** (top-left) to slide it out.

### The left-hand menu

Click any item to open it. The item you're currently in is highlighted. You won't
see *every* item below — the menu only shows what your role uses (and a few areas
are turned off for the trial).

| Menu item | What it's for | Who sees it during the trial |
|---|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Your home screen and daily snapshot. | Everyone |
| **Clients** | Find a client and open their record. | Everyone |
| **Calendar** | The appointment schedule (view and add sessions). | Everyone |
| **Messages** | The communication center for client and team messages. | Everyone |
| **Forms** | The library of forms — assign them and review what's sent back. | Everyone |
| **Treatment Plan Library** | Ready-made treatment plan templates to apply to a client. | Director, Therapist |
| **Compliance Risk** | A monitor of client alerts that may need attention. | Director, Therapist |
| **Settings** | Practice settings (under a **"Reports"** heading). | Director only |

> Note: the menu item is named **"Calendar,"** and it opens the scheduling page.

To shrink the menu to just icons, click the small button at the top of the menu;
click it again to expand. To leave the app, use **"Sign Out"** at the bottom.

### The top bar

- **Search box ("Search clinical resources...").** Click it (or press
  **Ctrl + K**) to open a quick **command palette**. Start typing in the
  **"Type a command or search..."** box to jump to a page like **"Dashboard"** or
  **"Clients,"** or to pull up a client by name.
- **The "Schedule" button (with a + ).** Your quick-create menu:
  - **"Schedule Session"** — open the new-appointment window.
  - **"New Intake"** — start a new client file.
  - **"Create Note"** — open the note assistant *(Director and Therapist only;
    Jessica won't see this one).*
- **Your profile picture (top-right).** Your account menu, including
  **"Settings"** (Director only) and **"Logout."**

A small trail of links (a "breadcrumb") near the top of most pages shows where you
are; click any part of it to step back.

### The "Clara" assistant (an early helper — please kick the tires)

In the bottom-right corner of every screen is a round button that opens
**Clara**, an AI assistant we're trying out. Click it (its label is
**"Open Clara AI Assistant"**), type a question in the **"Ask Clara about
today..."** box, and see what comes back. Close it with the Escape key or by
clicking the button again.

> 🧪 **Clara is brand-new and still learning.** Treat her as an experiment, not a
> finished tool — typing works best, and the voice feature may be rough. Please
> **only use made-up information** when trying her out, and tell us what felt
> helpful or off.

### A few things are turned off for the trial — that's expected

To keep the trial focused, three areas are intentionally hidden for now. **If you
don't see these, nothing is wrong:** **Financials**, **Analytics**, and
**AI Documents**. See [Help and what's next](#help-and-whats-next) for details.

## Common tasks

Short, click-by-click walkthroughs for the things you'll do most.

> ⚠️ **Trial reminder:** use **made-up, sample information** for every task below
> — no real client names or details yet.

### Add a client

*Available to everyone.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Clients."** You'll see the **"Select a Client"**
   screen.
2. Click the **"Add Client"** button (top-right). *(You can also use the top
   **"Schedule"** menu → **"New Intake."**)*
3. A window titled **"New Client Intake"** opens at **"Step 1 of 3 — Basic
   Demographics."** Fill in **First Name, Last Name, Phone Number, Email
   Address,** and **Date of Birth.** (Name and phone are required.)
4. Click **"Next"** to reach **"Program & Legal."** Choose a **"Program Track"**
   (for example, *"SROP (SATOP Recidivism Reduction)," "SATOP Level IV,"
   "REACT,"* or *"Anger Management"*), then enter **Case Number, County,** and
   **Probation Officer.**
5. Click **"Next"** to reach **"Billing Setup."** Pick a **"Payment Source"**
   (Self-Pay, Insurance, or Court Mandate) and review the **"Ready for Intake"**
   summary.
6. Click **"Create Client File."** The button briefly reads **"Creating..."**,
   then the new client's record opens automatically.

> Use **"Back"** to revisit an earlier step. If a required field is missing,
> you'll see a short message and be returned to Step 1.

### Open a client's record

*Available to everyone.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Clients."**
2. Find your client: pick a program from the **"Program"** dropdown (All, SATOP,
   REACT, Gambling Recovery, Opioid Recovery) and/or type in the **"Search by
   name or case #"** box.
3. Click the client's **card.** Their record opens on the **"Overview"** tab.
4. Move between tabs along the top: **Overview, Documents, Forms, Sessions** (and
   **Treatment Plan** if you're a Director or Therapist).

**To update a client's details:**

1. In the client's header, click **"Edit"** (the pencil).
2. In the **"Edit Client"** window, update the **Contact** fields and the
   **"Billing & Legal"** fields. *(The **"Clinical"** section is editable only for
   Director/Therapist.)*
3. Click **"Save Changes."**

### View the schedule

*Available to everyone.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Calendar."**
2. You'll see a **weekly grid.** Use the **"Today"** button to jump to this week,
   or the **left/right arrows** to move between weeks.
3. Each appointment shows as a **card** with the client or session name and the
   time. A card may show a **"Virtual"** badge (for video sessions) or a
   **"Synced to Google"** link.

### Schedule a session

*Available to everyone.*

1. On the **Calendar**, click **"Schedule Session"** (or use the top
   **"Schedule"** menu → **"Schedule Session"**).
2. In the **"Schedule New Session"** window, pick a **"Session Type"** — for
   example *SATOP Group, REACT Group, Anger Management Group, Gambling Group,
   Individual Counseling, DOT Assessment,* or *Intake Assessment.*
3. For an individual type, choose the **"Client."** For a group type, set the
   **"Capacity."**
4. Set the **"Date," "Start Time,"** and **"End Time."** The **"Therapist"** is
   already filled in for you. *(Helpful notes about travel time or a calendar
   conflict are just advisories and won't stop you.)*
5. Click **"Create Session."** The appointment appears on the weekly grid.

### Cancel a session

*Available to everyone.*

1. On the **Calendar**, **hover your mouse over the appointment** card.
2. A small **"X"** appears in the card's corner (labeled **"Cancel session"**).
   Click it.
3. A confirmation asks something like *"Cancel '…'? This will also remove the
   Google Calendar event and notify attendees."* Click **OK** to confirm.
4. The appointment is removed from the schedule.

> 💡 To **reschedule,** cancel the old appointment and create a new one at the new
> time.
>
> ℹ️ **Heads-up:** marking an appointment as **Completed** or **No-Show** isn't
> part of this version yet — for now, scheduling and canceling are the two
> actions. Fuller status tracking is on the list for after the trial.

### Apply a treatment plan

*Director and Therapist only.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Treatment Plan Library."**
2. Browse the ready-made templates. Narrow the list with the **"Search templates
   by title, description, or problem..."** box or the category chips (**All,
   SATOP, Gambling Recovery, Opioid Recovery, Anger Management, Mental Health**).
3. On the template you want, click **"Use This Template."** The **"Customize &
   Apply Plan"** window opens.
4. Under **"Apply to client,"** choose the client from the dropdown. *(If you
   started from a client's Treatment Plan tab, they're already selected.)*
5. Adjust the **"Plan title"** and **"Estimated duration"** if you like. Under
   **"Problems & goals,"** edit the text, use **"Add problem"** to add one, and
   within each problem add a **"Goal"** or **"Intervention."** You can add
   **"Clinical notes (optional)."**
6. Click **"Save plan."** If the client already has an active plan, you'll be
   asked to confirm with **"Archive existing & apply."**
7. The client's **"Treatment Plan"** tab opens showing the new plan marked
   **"Active."** From here you can **"Edit," "Archive,"** or start a **"New
   plan."** Older plans appear under **"Plan history."**

### Assign a form

*Available to everyone.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Forms."** The **"Form Templates"** tab is shown by
   default.
2. Find the form you need. Use the **"Search forms..."** box or the category
   pills (**All, Intake, Assessment, Treatment, Legal, Clinical**). Examples
   include *"Consent for Treatment," "Continuing Recovery Plan," "SATOP Client
   Intake,"* and *"Emergency Contact."*
3. On that form's card, click **"Assign."** The **"Assign Form"** window opens
   with the **"Form Template"** already chosen.
4. If a client checklist appears (**"Assign to Clients"**), check the clients you
   want — or use **"Select All."**
5. Set the **"Due Date"** (it defaults to one week out).
6. Click **"Assign Form to … Client(s)."** The assignment now appears under the
   **"Client Submissions"** tab.

> 👀 **Just want to look at a form first?** Click the small **info icon** on a
> form's card to preview it, then **"Acknowledge"** to close. Clients fill out the
> forms you assign; staff mainly **assign** and **review** them.

### Review form submissions

*Available to everyone.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Forms,"** then open the **"Client Submissions"**
   tab.
2. At the top are three counts: **"Awaiting Client," "Needs Review,"** and
   **"Reviewed."** You can filter the list with the buttons **all / pending /
   completed / reviewed,** or search with **"Search by client or form..."**
3. In the table (columns **Client, Form, Date, Status, Actions**), find the row
   you want. Submitted forms show a **"Review"** button (already-reviewed ones
   show **"View"**).
4. Click **"Review."** A window opens showing the answers under **"Form
   Responses."**
5. If the form is ready, click **"Mark as Reviewed."** Otherwise click
   **"Close."**

## Help and what's next

### What's coming after the trial

A few areas are intentionally **switched off or kept early** during these 30 days
so you can focus on the essentials. Here's the honest picture:

- **Financials** — a billing and revenue view for the Director. *Turned off for
  the trial.*
- **Analytics** — reports and charts (e.g., revenue by program, compliance
  trends). *Turned off for the trial.*
- **AI Documents** — tools to scan and read handwritten forms and uploaded
  paperwork. *Turned off for the trial.*
- **Fuller appointment tracking** — marking sessions **Completed** or **No-Show**
  and doing attendance check-in. *Not in this version yet; scheduling and
  canceling work today.*
- **Real sign-in for everyone** — adding your phone numbers to iVALT and moving
  from sample data to real client records. *We'll do this together.*
- **More polished AI helpers** — **Clara** and the **Clinical Smart Note Studio**
  are early experiments now; your trial feedback will guide where they go next.

> Nothing above is broken — it's simply not part of the trial.

### How to give feedback (this is the whole point!)

Quick notes are perfect. When something stands out, jot down:

1. **What you were trying to do** (e.g., "add a new client").
2. **What you expected to happen.**
3. **What actually happened** (and a screenshot if it's easy).
4. **How it felt** — confusing, slow, just right, delightful, anything.

Little things count: a button you couldn't find, a label that didn't make sense,
a step that felt like one too many.

During the trial, please keep using **made-up, sample information** — no real
client names or details — particularly with the AI helpers (Clara and the Smart
Note Studio).
