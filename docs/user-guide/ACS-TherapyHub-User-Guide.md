# Welcome to ACS TherapyHub

Welcome! ACS TherapyHub is the online home base for the day-to-day work of
Assessment & Counseling Solutions — your clients, your schedule, your forms, and
your treatment plans, all in one place. You open it in a web browser and sign in;
there is nothing to install.

This guide is here to help you get comfortable on your own during the **30-day
trial**, *before* we sit down together to fine-tune things. You don't need any
technical background. Read what's useful, skip what isn't, and click around — you
can't break anything.

## This is a trial, so please explore (and tell us what you think)

The trial has two goals:

1. **Get you logged in and looking around** so the app feels familiar.
2. **Gather your honest reactions.** What's clear? What's confusing? What's
   missing? Your feedback during these 30 days directly shapes how we set the
   app up for your practice.

Throughout this guide you'll see **"Tell us what you think"** notes. Those are
gentle nudges to jot down a reaction and pass it along (see
[Help & what's next](07-help-and-whats-next.md)).

# Getting started: signing in

When you open ACS TherapyHub, the first thing you'll see is the sign-in screen.
It shows the ACS logo, the title **"ACS TherapyHub"**, and the line
*"Clinical operations for Assessment & Counseling Solutions."*

There are **two ways to sign in**. During the trial, the **demo access** option
is the quickest, because it doesn't need a registered phone.

---

## Option A — Demo access (easiest for the trial)

Use this to look around right away.

1. On the sign-in screen, click **"Continue with demo access"**.
2. You'll see a short list headed **"Enter the demo as"** with three choices.
   Each is a role with a name and a one-line description:
   - **Director** — David Yoder — *"Full access — clinical, financial, settings."*
   - **Therapist** — Karen — *"Clinical work — notes, treatment plans, sessions."*
   - **Admin** — Jessica — *"Office work — intake, scheduling, billing."*
3. Click the card that matches you. You'll go straight to your **Dashboard** —
   no phone needed.

> Want to see what a teammate sees? Sign out and come back in as a different
> role. It's a great way to understand who can do what. To return to the phone
> sign-in, click **"Back to sign in"**.

---

## Option B — Phone sign-in with iVALT (the secure everyday method)

This is how you'll normally sign in once your phone number is set up. Instead of
a password, you approve the sign-in on your phone with a face/biometric check
through the **iVALT** app.

1. In the box labeled **"Phone — used for sign-in approval"**, type your
   **10-digit mobile number**. (A **"+1"** is already shown for you.)
2. Click **"Sign in to ACS TherapyHub"**.
3. A secure window titled **"iVALT SECURE"** appears and asks you to
   **"Check your phone now."**
4. On your phone, approve the request with iVALT's face scan.
5. When it succeeds, the window turns green and reads **"ACCESS GRANTED"**, and
   you're taken to your **Dashboard**.

If you need to stop partway through, click **"TERMINATE HANDSHAKE"**. If a
sign-in doesn't go through, you'll see a **"Retry"** button to try again.

### If something doesn't work

- **"Enter a 10-digit phone number registered with iVALT."** — The number wasn't
  exactly 10 digits. Re-type it and try again.
- **"...this phone number is not registered to a TherapyHub account. Contact your
  administrator."** — Your phone approved, but it isn't on file yet. Reach out to
  your contact (see [Help & what's next](07-help-and-whats-next.md)) to get your
  number added. In the meantime, use **demo access** (Option A).

## Add a client

*Available to everyone.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Clients."** You'll see the **"Select a Client"**
   screen.
2. Click the **"Add Client"** button (top-right). *(You can also use the top
   **"Schedule"** menu → **"New Intake."**)*
3. A window titled **"New Client Intake"** opens at **"Step 1 of 3 — Basic
   Demographics."** Fill in **First Name, Last Name, Phone Number, Email
   Address,** and **Date of Birth.** (Name and phone are required.)
4. Click **"Next"** to reach **"Program & Legal."** Choose a **"Program Track"**
   (*SROP (SATOP Recidivism Reduction), SATOP Level IV, REACT,* or *Anger
   Management*), then enter a **Case Number,** pick a **County,** and enter a
   **Probation Officer.**
5. Click **"Next"** to reach **"Billing Setup."** Pick a **"Payment Source"**
   (Self-Pay, Insurance, or Court Mandate) and review the **"Ready for Intake"**
   summary.
6. Click **"Create Client File."** The button briefly reads **"Creating..."**,
   then the new client's record opens automatically.

> Use **"Back"** to revisit an earlier step. If a required field is missing, you'll
> see a short message (like *"First and last name are required."*) and be returned
> to the first step.

## Open a client's record

2. Find your client: pick a program from the **"Program"** dropdown (All, SATOP,
   REACT, Gambling Recovery, Opioid Recovery) and/or type in the **"Search by

1. In the client's header, click **"Edit"** (the pencil).
2. In the **"Edit Client"** window, update the **Contact** fields and the
   **"Billing & Legal"** fields. *(The **"Clinical"** section is editable only for
   Director/Therapist; the office role sees a **"Therapist / Director only"** label
   there.)*
3. Click **"Save Changes."**

## Schedule a session

*Available to everyone.*

1. On the **Calendar**, click **"Schedule Session"** (or use the top
   **"Schedule"** menu → **"Schedule Session"**).
2. In the **"Schedule New Session"** window, pick a **"Session Type"** — for
   example *SATOP Group, REACT Group, Anger Management Group, Gambling Group,
   Individual Counseling, DOT Assessment,* or *Intake Assessment.*
3. For an individual type, choose the **"Client."** For a group type, set the
   **"Capacity."** *(If your practice has standing groups set up, you can pick one
   and it will fill in the counselor and meeting room for you.)*
4. Set the **"Date," "Start Time,"** and **"End Time."** The **"Therapist"** is
   already filled in for you. *(You may see helpful notes about travel time or a
   calendar conflict — these are just advisories and won't stop you.)*
5. Click **"Create Session."** The appointment appears on the weekly grid.

## Update a session: complete, no-show, or cancel

*Available to everyone.*

Clicking any appointment card on the **Calendar** opens the **"Appointment
Status"** window. From there you can record what happened:

**Mark a session complete (this is how program hours add up):**

1. Click the appointment card.
2. Choose a **"Session category"** — *Counseling, Education,* or *Group
   rehabilitative support.* (A category is required before you can complete a
   session; it's how the hours are counted toward the client's program.)
3. Click **"Mark Completed."** The card turns green.

**Mark a no-show:**

1. Click the appointment card.
2. Click **"Mark No-Show."** The card is marked accordingly.

**Cancel a session:**

1. Click the appointment card, then **"Cancel Session."**
2. If the start time is **more than 24 hours away,** the session is simply
   canceled.
3. If it's **less than 24 hours away,** a panel appears explaining that, per the
   ACS Late Cancellation Policy, a late-cancellation fee applies. You can:
   - **"Assess … fee & cancel"** — the fee posts to the client's billing ledger, or
   - **"Waive (emergency)…"** — enter a short reason (for example, a documented
     medical emergency), then confirm.
4. Canceling **keeps** the appointment on the record (it's just marked Canceled).

> To **reschedule,** cancel the old appointment and
> [create a new one](#schedule-a-session) at the new time. If you set a status by
> mistake, reopen the card and click **"Reset to Scheduled."** **"Delete
> permanently"** removes the appointment entirely (and its Google Calendar event) —
> use it only when an appointment shouldn't exist at all.

1. In the left menu, click **"Treatment Plan Library."**
2. Browse the ready-made templates. Narrow the list with the **"Search templates
   by title, description, or problem..."** box or the category chips (**All,
   SATOP, Gambling Recovery, Opioid Recovery, Anger Management, Mental Health**).
3. On the template you want, click **"Use This Template."** The **"Customize &
   Apply Plan"** window opens.
4. Under **"Apply to client,"** choose the client from the dropdown. *(If you
   started from a client's Treatment Plan tab, they're already selected.)*
5. Adjust the **"Plan title"** and **"Estimated duration"** if you like. Under
   **"Problems & goals,"** edit the text, use **"Add problem"** to add one, and
   within each problem add a **"Goal"** or **"Intervention."** You can add
   **"Clinical notes (optional)."**
6. Click **"Save plan."** If the client already has an active plan, you'll be
   asked to confirm with **"Archive existing & apply."**
7. The client's **"Treatment Plan"** tab opens showing the new plan marked
   **"Active."** From here you can **"Edit," "Archive,"** or start a **"New
   plan."** Older plans appear under **"Plan history."**

## Assign a form

*Available to everyone.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Forms."** The **"Form Templates"** tab is shown by
   default.
2. Find the form you need. Use the **"Search forms..."** box or the category
   pills (**All, Intake, Assessment, Treatment, Legal, Clinical**). Examples
   include *"Consent for Treatment," "Continuing Recovery Plan," "SATOP Client
   Intake,"* and *"Emergency Contact."*
3. On that form's card, click **"Assign."** The **"Assign Form"** window opens
   with the **"Form Template"** already chosen.
4. If a client checklist appears (**"Assign to Clients"**), check the clients you
   want — or use **"Select All."**
5. Set the **"Due Date"** (it defaults to one week out).
6. Click **"Assign Form to … Client(s)."** The assignment now appears under the
   **"Client Submissions"** tab.

> **Just want to look at a form first?** Click the small **info icon** on a form's
> card to preview it, then **"Acknowledge"** to close. Clients fill out the forms
> you assign; staff mainly **assign** and **review** them.

## Review form submissions

*Available to everyone.*

1. In the left menu, click **"Forms,"** then open the **"Client Submissions"**
   tab.
2. At the top are three counts: **"Awaiting Client," "Needs Review,"** and
   **"Reviewed."** You can filter the list with the buttons **all / pending /
   completed / reviewed,** or search with **"Search by client or form..."**
3. In the table (columns **Client, Form, Date, Status, Actions**), find the row
   you want. Submitted forms show a **"Review"** button (already-reviewed ones
   show **"View"**).
4. Click **"Review."** A window opens showing the answers under **"Form
   Responses."**
5. If the form is ready, click **"Mark as Reviewed."** Otherwise click
   **"Close."**

## How to give feedback (this is the whole point)

Your reactions during the trial are what make the real setup go smoothly. You
don't need to write a report — quick notes are perfect. When something stands
out, jot down:

1. **What you were trying to do** (e.g., "add a new client").
2. **What you expected to happen.**
3. **What actually happened** (and a screenshot if it's easy).
4. **How it felt** — confusing, slow, just right, anything.

Little things count: a button you couldn't find, a label that didn't make sense,
a step that felt like one too many. Send your notes to your contact below.

> Especially welcome: anything that made you stop and think "wait, how do I…?"
> Those moments are gold for improving the setup.
