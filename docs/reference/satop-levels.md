# SATOP Program Levels — Quick Reference

> **Educational reference only.** This explains how Missouri's SATOP levels work. It does **not** report any specific client's compliance status — that is determined by the system from each client's actual record.

The Substance Awareness Traffic Offender Program (SATOP) is Missouri's education-and-intervention system for alcohol- and drug-related driving offenses. Completing the assigned level is a state requirement for license reinstatement through the Department of Revenue. Every in-state client enters through an **Offender Management Unit (OMU) screening** conducted by a SATOP Qualified Professional, which determines the assigned level.

*Authority: Missouri Department of Mental Health, Division of Behavioral Health — 9 CSR 30-3.*

---

## The five levels at a glance

**ADEP — Adolescent Diversion Education Program**
For underage drivers (under 21) cited for Minor in Possession, Abuse and Lose, or Zero Tolerance violations. Basic substance-use education and decision-making. **10 hours.**

**OEP — Offender Education Program (Level I)**
For low-risk, first-time offenders with no clinical indicators of a substance use disorder. Impaired-driving law, physiological effects, personal responsibility. **10 hours**, completed within a 6-month window.

**WIP — Weekend Intervention Program (Level II)**
For repeat offenders or higher-risk first-time offenders. Intensive education and group counseling in a restrictive weekend setting. **20 continuous hours** over a 48-hour weekend.

**CIP — Clinical Intervention Program (Level III)**
For offenders meeting diagnostic criteria for a mild-to-moderate substance use disorder. **50 hours total**, split as:
- 10 hours individual counseling
- 20 hours group counseling
- 20 hours group education
- with **at least 10 hours** focused specifically on impaired-driving issues.

Delivered by a Qualified Substance Abuse Professional (QSAP).

**SROP — Serious and Repeat Offender Program (Level IV)**
The most intensive level — for serious or persistent offenders, often with multiple offenses or a higher BAC. A multi-phase outpatient program with external sober-support participation. **Minimum 75 hours, delivered over no less than 90 calendar days.** Both the hours *and* the 90-day minimum must be met. Delivered by a QSAP.

## Comparable out-of-state programs

A client who received a Missouri offense but lives elsewhere (or relocates mid-program) may complete a **comparable program** in their home state instead of standard SATOP. The requirements:

- A **certified or accredited** out-of-state provider (e.g., state-certified, Joint Commission, or CARF).
- Completed **within 6 months** of the initial out-of-state assessment.
- A minimum of **120 total treatment hours**, including at least **40 hours of counseling** and at least **10 hours of drug-and-alcohol education**; remaining hours from driver education, group education, or family therapy.
- The **SATOP Comparable Program Completion Form** (Sections II, III, IV) completed by the out-of-state clinician.
- The **$249 supplemental fee** submitted to the Missouri Department of Mental Health.

Out-of-state clinicians are often unfamiliar with Missouri's specific forms, so careful tracking of the form sections and fee is what prevents rejection.
