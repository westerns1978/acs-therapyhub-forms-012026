# Clinical Credentials & Scope — Quick Reference

> **Educational reference only.** This summarizes who can do what under Missouri's staffing rules. It does **not** reflect any individual staff member's verified credentials in the system — that is managed in each user's profile.

Missouri regulations (9 CSR 30-3.151, 30-3.155, 10-7.140) tie specific clinical actions to specific credentials. The short version: **diagnosis and final treatment-plan sign-off are the most restricted actions**, and several roles can *draft* but must route to a licensed professional to *finalize*.

---

## Who can do what

**LMHP — Licensed Mental Health Professional**
Licensed Physician, Psychiatrist, Psychologist, APRN, LPC, LMFT, or LCSW.
Can render DSM-5-TR diagnoses, perform comprehensive assessments, and **approve and sign treatment plans.** Can act as a clinical supervisor. The only role authorized for fully independent diagnostic and sign-off work.

**Provisional LMHP — PLPC / LMSW**
Can *draft* diagnoses, conduct intake assessments, and *draft* treatment plans — but diagnostics and plans must be **co-signed by a licensed LMHP.** (An LMSW may render diagnoses only while under registered supervision toward LCSW; unsupervised LMSWs cannot.)

**QAP / QMHP — Qualified Addiction / Mental Health Professional**
MCB-certified (CADC, CRADC, CRAADC, CCJP, CCDP, CCDP-D). Can conduct intake, develop treatment plans, and facilitate therapy — but **cannot render an independent DSM-5-TR diagnosis.** Must route the clinical summary to an LMHP for final signature (a dual-signature workflow).

**SQP / SQP-R — SATOP Qualified Professional**
MCB-credentialed. Conducts **OMU screening assessments**, assigns SATOP levels, and facilitates OEP and WIP. OMU screenings are done independently; WIP individual sessions require supervisor oversight.

**QSAP — Qualified Substance Abuse Professional**
Required to **deliver the intensive outpatient levels** (CIP, YCIP, SROP). A physician, a licensed QMHP with 1+ year SUD experience, or an MCB-certified/registered professional.

**Associate Counselor — RASAC I / RASAC II**
Can deliver individual counseling, group counseling, and group education — but **only under the direct oversight and full professional responsibility** of a trained clinical supervisor.

---

## The two patterns worth remembering

1. **Diagnosis is LMHP-only.** Everyone else either can't diagnose, or can only draft-then-route for co-signature.
2. **Intensive outpatient (CIP/SROP) requires a QSAP** delivering it; associates can help only under supervision.
